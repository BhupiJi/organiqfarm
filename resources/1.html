Composting 101: Create Nutrient-Rich Soil in 30 Days

Turn kitchen scraps and yard waste into black gold for your garden with this complete beginner's guide.

Why Compost?

Composting is nature's way of recycling organic material into rich soil amendment. It reduces landfill waste, improves soil structure, and provides essential nutrients to your plants. Best of all, it's completely free!

Quick Start Tip

Start small with a simple pile or bin. You don't need fancy equipment to begin composting effectively.

Three Easy Composting Methods

1. Hot Composting

Time: 1-3 months
Effort: Medium
Best for: Large amounts of material

Fastest method using heat from microbial activity. Requires regular turning and proper carbon-nitrogen balance.

2. Vermicomposting

Time: 2-4 months
Effort: Low
Best for: Apartments, small spaces

Uses worms to break down organic matter. Perfect for indoor composting with minimal odor.

3. Bokashi

Time: 2-4 weeks
Effort: Low
Best for: All food waste

Anaerobic fermentation using beneficial microbes. Can process meat and dairy that other methods can't.
