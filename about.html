Why OrganiqFarm Exists

The year 2026 represents a critical inflection point for global agriculture. Climate pressures, soil degradation rates, and public health concerns have converged to create an unprecedented urgency for transformation. Yet, farmers, policymakers, and consumers lack access to unified, verified data that cuts through misinformation and special interests.

How This Site is Sustained

Transparency in our funding model ensures independence and integrity. We maintain strict ethical guidelines for all partnerships.

Affiliate Partnerships

Carefully selected links to organic seeds, tools, and books from ethical suppliers. A portion of qualifying sales supports our research without increasing costs for consumers.

Transparency: All affiliate links are clearly marked. We only partner with companies meeting our sustainability criteria.

Explore Partners
~40% of funding

Community Donations

Direct funding from individuals and organizations for specific data projects, soil testing initiatives, or community farm support programs.

Transparency: 100% of donations go to specified projects. We provide quarterly reports on how funds were used and outcomes achieved.

Make a Donation
~35% of funding

Sponsored Research

Partnerships with ethical institutions, universities, and NGOs to publish foundational studies. We maintain editorial independence while collaborating on data collection.

Transparency: All sponsored research is clearly labeled. We never alter findings to suit sponsors' interests.

Research Inquiry
~25% of funding

Our Ethical Guidelines

1. We never accept funding from synthetic pesticide or fertilizer manufacturers.
2. All data remains open-access for educational purposes.
3. Affiliate relationships don't influence our research priorities.
4. We disclose the methodology behind all our findings.
5. Conflicting interests are publicly disclosed.
